Ancient Roman Pork with Apples

Translated from Latin and adapted by Laura Kelley of The Silk Road Gourmet, Ancient Roman Pork with Apples is a way to use leftover pork. It calls for defrutum, grape juice that has been boiled down and made into a syrup, which was a common sweetener used in that time. It seems to be an example of the sweet and savory combo that Herbert spoke about. Kelley says the recipe "balances sweet, sour, salty and bitter" and the "unami factor is through the roof."

Ingredients

  • Pork Loin
  • Apples
  • Honey
  • Red Wine
  • Garum (fish sauce)
  • Black Pepper
  • Olive Oil
  • Dates (optional, for sweetness)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to the specified temperature.
  2. Season the pork loin with black pepper and a drizzle of olive oil.
  3. Place the seasoned pork loin in a roasting pan.
  4. Peel and slice the apples, then arrange them around the pork loin in the roasting pan.
  5. In a bowl, mix honey, red wine, and garum. Pour this mixture over the pork and apples.
  6. If desired, add chopped dates for sweetness.
  7. Roast in the preheated oven until the pork is cooked through and the apples are tender.
  8. Once done, remove from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing.
  9. Serve the Ancient Roman Pork with Apples, drizzling the pan juices over the slices.
  10. Enjoy your historical culinary adventure!
